BAYOMA:
Bayesian Operational Modal Analysis

Identify the dynamic properties of civil structures based on Bayesian fomulation

OUR GOAL

To identify the modal properties (natural frequencies, damping ratios, mode shapes, etc) of full-scale structures using in-situ vibration measurements (e.g., acceleration) at a limited number of locations and under ambient conditions.

There are already many methods that can obtain an estimate of the modal properties but questions remain on repeatability and accuracy. Contemporary challenges are related to the quality of estimates, their proper use to provide scientific evidence, effective design of experiments trading off required precision and practical constraints, industrial applications with sophistication commensurate with project objectives. A Bayesian probabilistic framework is advocated, in anticipation to provide a fundamental scientific yet practical solution to industrial problems.

CURRENT DIRECTIONS

- development of efficient algorithms for different contexts, e.g., well-separated modes (JEM 2011), general multiple (possibly close) modes (MSSP 2012), multiple setups (JEM 2012; SCHM 2015)

- 'uncertainty laws' (MSSP 2014) - closed form analytical expressions fundamentally quantifying the identification uncertainty of identified modal properties; revealing the scientific precision limits of operational modal analysis and providing the basis for planning ambient vibration tests.

- spin-off industrial applications, e.g., amplitude-dependent damping (WEIA 2012), full-scale calibration of wind tunnel test results (JSE 2012), electro-mechanical oscillation of power networks (wide-area monitoring, IEEE TPS 2016)
